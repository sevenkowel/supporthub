# Margin

## What is Margin?

Margin is the amount of money required to open and maintain a leveraged trading position. It's essentially a deposit or collateral that you need to provide to your broker to cover potential losses.

## How Margin Works

When trading forex with leverage, you only need to deposit a percentage of the total trade value. This deposit is called margin.

**Example:**
- You want to trade 1 lot of EUR/USD (100,000 units)
- Broker offers 100:1 leverage
- Required margin = 100,000 ÷ 100 = **1,000 units of base currency**

## Types of Margin

### 1. Initial Margin (Required Margin)
- The minimum amount needed to open a position
- Calculated based on leverage and trade size

### 2. Maintenance Margin
- The minimum equity required to keep a position open
- If your equity falls below this level, you may receive a margin call

### 3. Free Margin
- Available funds that can be used to open new positions
- Free Margin = Equity - Used Margin

## Margin Calculations

### Formula
```
Required Margin = (Trade Size × Current Price) ÷ Leverage
```

### Example Calculation
```
Trade: 1 lot GBP/USD (100,000 units)
Price: 1.2500
Leverage: 100:1

Required Margin = (100,000 × 1.2500) ÷ 100 = 1,250 USD
```

## Margin vs. Leverage

- **Margin** is the amount you need to deposit
- **Leverage** is the multiplier applied to your margin
- Higher leverage = Lower margin requirement
- Higher leverage = Higher risk

## Important Considerations

### 1. Margin Calls
If your account equity falls below the maintenance margin requirement, your broker may issue a margin call requiring you to deposit additional funds.

### 2. Stop Out Level
If your equity continues to fall and reaches the stop out level, your broker may automatically close your positions to prevent further losses.

### 3. Risk Management
Always ensure you have sufficient margin to withstand market fluctuations. Never risk more than you can afford to lose.
