# Spread

## What is Spread?

Spread is the difference between the bid price (selling price) and ask price (buying price) of a currency pair. It represents the transaction cost of trading and is measured in pips.

## Bid vs. Ask Prices

### Bid Price
- The price at which you can **sell** the base currency
- Also called the "sell price" or "offer price"
- Always lower than the ask price

### Ask Price
- The price at which you can **buy** the base currency
- Also called the "buy price"
- Always higher than the bid price

### Spread Calculation
```
Spread = Ask Price - Bid Price
```

**Example:**
- EUR/USD quote: 1.1000 / 1.1003
- Bid price: 1.1000
- Ask price: 1.1003
- Spread: 1.1003 - 1.1000 = **0.0003** (3 pips)

## Types of Spreads

### 1. Fixed Spreads
- Remain constant regardless of market conditions
- Typically offered by market maker brokers
- **Advantages**: Predictable costs, easier budgeting
- **Disadvantages**: May be wider than variable spreads in normal conditions

### 2. Variable Spreads (Floating Spreads)
- Change based on market conditions
- Typically offered by ECN/STP brokers
- **Advantages**: Tighter spreads during normal conditions
- **Disadvantages**: Can widen significantly during volatility

### 3. Commission-Based Spreads
- Very tight or zero spreads
- Broker charges separate commission per trade
- Common with ECN accounts

## Factors Affecting Spreads

### 1. Market Liquidity
- Major pairs (EUR/USD, GBP/USD) have tighter spreads
- Exotic pairs have wider spreads
- High liquidity = tighter spreads

### 2. Trading Volume
- Higher volume periods often have tighter spreads
- Lower volume (nights, weekends) can widen spreads

### 3. Market Volatility
- High volatility (news events) widens spreads
- Low volatility periods often have tighter spreads

### 4. Broker Type
- Market makers: Fixed or slightly variable spreads
- ECN brokers: Variable spreads reflecting market conditions
- STP brokers: Variable spreads from liquidity providers

### 5. Account Type
- Standard accounts: Typical spreads
- Premium/VIP accounts: Tighter spreads
- Commission accounts: Raw spreads + separate commission

## Spread Measurements

### Pips
- Most common measurement
- Fourth decimal place for most pairs (0.0001)
- Second decimal place for JPY pairs (0.01)

### Points (Pipettes)
- Fifth decimal place (0.00001)
- Used for more precise measurement
- 10 points = 1 pip

### Percentage
- Spread as percentage of price
- Useful for comparing across different instruments

## Calculating Trading Costs

### Cost Formula
```
Cost = Spread (in pips) × Pip Value × Lot Size
```

### Example Calculation
```
Trade: 1 lot EUR/USD (100,000 units)
Spread: 2 pips (0.0002)
Pip value for 1 lot: $10

Cost = 2 pips × $10 = $20
```

This means you start the trade $20 in the negative (breakeven at +2 pips).

## How Spreads Affect Trading

### 1. Trading Strategy Compatibility
- **Scalping**: Requires very tight spreads
- **Day trading**: Moderately tight spreads acceptable
- **Swing trading**: Wider spreads less impactful
- **Position trading**: Spreads relatively unimportant

### 2. Profitability Impact
- Tighter spreads reduce trading costs
- Lower costs = higher potential profitability
- Particularly important for high-frequency strategies

### 3. Stop Loss Placement
- Must account for spread when setting stops
- Stop should be beyond spread width to avoid premature triggering

## Spread Comparison by Currency Pair

### Major Pairs (Tightest Spreads)
- **EUR/USD**: 0.5-1.5 pips typically
- **GBP/USD**: 1-2 pips typically
- **USD/JPY**: 0.7-1.5 pips typically
- **AUD/USD**: 1-2 pips typically

### Minor Pairs (Crosses)
- **EUR/GBP**: 1-3 pips typically
- **EUR/JPY**: 1-3 pips typically
- **GBP/JPY**: 2-4 pips typically

### Exotic Pairs (Widest Spreads)
- **USD/TRY**: 10-50 pips typically
- **EUR/TRY**: 15-60 pips typically
- **USD/ZAR**: 15-40 pips typically

## Broker Spread Models

### Market Maker Model
- Broker acts as counterparty
- Often offers fixed spreads
- May have dealing desk intervention

### ECN Model
- Direct access to interbank market
- Variable spreads based on liquidity
- Usually commission-based

### STP Model
- Straight Through Processing
- Orders routed to liquidity providers
- Variable spreads, no dealing desk

### Hybrid Model
- Combination of models
- May offer both fixed and variable spread accounts

## Tips for Managing Spread Costs

### 1. Choose the Right Account Type
- High-frequency traders: ECN/commission accounts
- Beginners/small accounts: Fixed spread accounts
- Large volume traders: VIP accounts with tighter spreads

### 2. Trade During Optimal Hours
- Overlap of major sessions (London-NY) has tightest spreads
- Avoid trading during low-liquidity periods
- Be aware of news release times

### 3. Select Appropriate Currency Pairs
- Stick to majors for tight spreads
- Avoid exotics unless you understand the costs
- Consider spreads when choosing pairs to trade

### 4. Monitor Spread Changes
- Some brokers widen spreads significantly during news
- Check broker's spread policy
- Use platforms that show real-time spread information

### 5. Factor Spread into Risk Management
- Include spread in position sizing calculations
- Adjust stop losses for spread width
- Consider spread when calculating risk/reward ratios

## Regulatory Considerations

### Best Execution Requirements
Brokers must ensure clients receive best possible execution, including:
- Best available prices (including spreads)
- Reasonable costs
- Fast execution

### Transparency Requirements
Brokers must disclose:
- Typical spread ranges for each instrument
- Conditions when spreads may widen
- Any additional costs or commissions
