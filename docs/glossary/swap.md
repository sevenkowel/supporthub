# Swap

## What is Swap?

Swap, also known as rollover interest or overnight financing charge, is the interest paid or earned for holding a position overnight. In forex trading, you're essentially borrowing one currency to buy another, and swap represents the interest rate differential between the two currencies.

## How Swap Works

When you hold a position past the broker's daily cut-off time (usually 5 PM New York time, or 21:00 GMT), you either pay or receive swap interest based on:

1. **The interest rates of the two currencies**
2. **Your position direction (long or short)**
3. **The size of your position**

### Interest Rate Differential
Each currency has an associated interest rate set by its central bank. The swap is calculated based on the difference between these rates.

## Swap Calculation

### Basic Formula
```
Swap = (Position Size × Interest Rate Differential × Days Held) ÷ 365
```

### For Forex
```
Swap = Lots × Pip Value × Swap Rate
```

Where:
- **Swap Rate**: Published by broker (in pips)
- **Positive**: You earn interest
- **Negative**: You pay interest

## Types of Swap

### 1. Long Position Swap
- When you **buy** (go long) a currency pair
- You earn interest on the currency you bought
- You pay interest on the currency you sold

### 2. Short Position Swap
- When you **sell** (go short) a currency pair
- You earn interest on the currency you sold
- You pay interest on the currency you bought

## Determining Swap Direction

### Rule of Thumb
- **Long higher interest rate currency**: Earn swap
- **Short higher interest rate currency**: Pay swap
- **Long lower interest rate currency**: Pay swap
- **Short lower interest rate currency**: Earn swap

### Example: AUD/USD
```
AUD interest rate: 4.35%
USD interest rate: 5.50%
Differential: USD 1.15% higher

Long AUD/USD: Pay swap (long lower rate currency)
Short AUD/USD: Earn swap (short higher rate currency)
```

## Factors Affecting Swap Rates

### 1. Central Bank Interest Rates
- Primary determinant of swap rates
- Changes when central banks adjust rates

### 2. Broker Markup
- Brokers add markup to interbank rates
- Markup varies by broker and account type
- Islamic accounts may have different swap policies

### 3. Market Conditions
- Liquidity can affect swap rates slightly
- Generally stable but can change with monetary policy

### 4. Position Size
- Swap is proportional to position size
- Larger positions = larger swap amounts

### 5. Days Held
- Standard: Charged daily
- Wednesday special: Triple swap (covers weekend)
- Holidays: May affect calculation

## Wednesday Triple Swap

### Why Triple Swap?
Forex markets close Friday 5 PM EST and reopen Sunday 5 PM EST. To account for the weekend:

- Positions held Wednesday night incur **3 days** of swap
- Covers Wednesday, Saturday, and Sunday
- No trading on Saturday/Sunday, so interest is charged in advance

### Exception
Some brokers use Thursday for triple swap. Check your broker's specific policy.

## Swap in Practice

### Broker Display
Brokers typically show:
- **Swap long**: Rate for long positions
- **Swap short**: Rate for short positions
- In pips or monetary amount per lot

### Example Display
```
EUR/USD
Swap Long: -0.75 pips (pay)
Swap Short: +0.25 pips (earn)
```

## Trading Implications

### 1. Carry Trade Strategy
- Strategy based on earning swap
- Buy high-interest currency, sell low-interest currency
- Earn both potential appreciation and swap
- **Risks**: Currency depreciation can outweigh swap earnings

### 2. Day Trading vs. Swing Trading
- **Day traders**: Avoid swap by closing before cut-off
- **Swing traders**: Must factor swap into cost calculations
- **Position traders**: Swap significant over long periods

### 3. Cost Considerations
- For long-term trades, swap can be substantial
- Must be included in profit/loss calculations
- Can turn winning trades into losers over time

## Calculating Swap Costs/Earnings

### Example 1: Earning Swap
```
Pair: AUD/JPY (long)
Position: 1 standard lot (100,000 units)
Swap rate: +2.5 pips daily
Pip value: ~$7 (varies with price)

Daily swap: 2.5 × $7 = $17.50 earned
Monthly (20 days): $350 earned
```

### Example 2: Paying Swap
```
Pair: USD/JPY (long)  
Position: 2 standard lots (200,000 units)
Swap rate: -1.8 pips daily
Pip value: ~$8

Daily swap: 1.8 × $8 × 2 = $28.80 paid
Monthly (20 days): $576 paid
```

## Special Account Types

### Islamic Accounts (Swap-Free)
- Offered to comply with Sharia law
- No swap charges or payments
- May have alternative fees (administration charges)
- Often require verification of religious requirements

### Professional Accounts
- May have different swap rates
- Sometimes better rates for larger clients
- Check broker's terms for each account type

## Managing Swap

### 1. Monitor Swap Rates
- Check rates before entering long-term positions
- Rates change with central bank decisions
- Some brokers offer swap rate calendars

### 2. Time Your Entries/Exits
- Enter after swap charged if paying swap
- Exit before swap charged if paying swap
- Consider Wednesday triple swap

### 3. Consider Swap in Strategy
- Factor swap into risk/reward calculations
- For carry trades, ensure swap justifies risk
- Avoid negative swap for long-term positions unless strategy-specific

### 4. Use Appropriate Account Type
- Day traders: Standard account fine
- Islamic traders: Swap-free account
- Carry traders: Account with competitive swap rates

## Regulatory Considerations

### Transparency Requirements
Brokers must clearly disclose:
- Swap calculation method
- Current swap rates
- When swaps are charged
- Any special conditions (Wednesday triple)

### Best Execution
- Swap rates should be competitive
- No hidden swap-related charges
- Clear policies for all account types

## Common Questions

### Q: Do I pay swap on demo accounts?
**A**: Usually yes, to simulate real trading conditions, but check broker policy.

### Q: Can swap rates change?
**A**: Yes, with central bank rate changes. Brokers typically update monthly.

### Q: Is swap charged on weekends?
**A**: No trading Saturday/Sunday, but triple swap Wednesday covers weekend.

### Q: Do all instruments have swap?
**A**: Most do. Stocks, indices, commodities also have financing charges.

### Q: How to find current swap rates?
**A**: Broker platform, website, or trading specifications document.
